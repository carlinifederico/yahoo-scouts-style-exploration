Delivery 01 !

First Style Exploration

Our opening round cast a wide net — soft 3D mascots, flat cartoons, expressive portraits and geometric takes — to find the character's DNA.

Client feedback

Keep it simple and legible

  • Should stay legible at small sizes, so a bit simpler.
  • Avoid anything too cartoonish or childish.
  • Drawn to the clearer shapes — especially the light-blue and purple ones.

Client leaned toward the cleaner, simpler soft mascots.

Delivery 02 !

Second Style Exploration

We split the difference between high-energy comic portraits and a cleaner, more editorial system — and tested soft 3D / Memoji-style faces.

Client feedback

More editorial, less comic book

  • Anchor back to the Yahoo brand — its history and its future.
  • Think more editorial than comic book (Notion is a good reference).
  • Design for screens & products first; it has to shine at small size.
  • More modern, sleek and future-facing (à la Apple) while keeping the Yahoo wink.
  • Still feeling a touch juvenile — consider emoji / Memoji territory.
  • References: Duolingo's floating face at small sizes, color combos from Games, Headspace.

Directions that moved toward the brief — editorial flats and soft Memoji-style faces.

Delivery 03 !

Third Style Exploration

A flat, high-contrast illustration system with limited palettes — the round the client called "beautiful explorations."

Client feedback

Bold, high-contrast, ownable

  • #5 is the most successful — closest to our illustration style, simple and high-contrast.
  • Interested in pushing #10 & #15 for the ownable quality they bring to the base character.
  • A closer connection to the brand: bold, high contrast, limited color palettes, clever.
  • Steer away from heavy gradient use; keep high contrast so it reads at small sizes.
  • Avoid drifting into generic "corporate Memphis" territory.

The client named #5, #10 and #15, and favored the red-cap, green-cap and yellow characters.
